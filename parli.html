What is Parliamentary Debate?

Parliamentary debate, originating from British parliament, is the oldest and most popular form of competitive debate around the world despite being relatively new to American high school circuits. A parliamentary debate round consists of two teams of two debaters, one affirming the topic and the other negating. Parliâ€™s unique feature is its varying resolution every round. Prior to the start of the round, a resolution is announced and both teams have twenty minutes to prepare its side of the case. As a result, participants accrue a wide breadth of knowledge regarding a multitude of domestic and international issues as well as a deep understanding of economics and government.
